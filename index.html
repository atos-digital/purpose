Empowered Developers

  • Culture

Culture

  • Nice people
    • Nice people are easier to work with
    • Nice people lift others up, and are easier to lift up
    • Nice people reject negative cultures like hero and bro cultures
  • Passionate and able people
    • Passionate people come with initiative included
    • Passionate people take ownership of all aspects of thier work
    • Passionate people teach themselves, they are lifelong learners
  • Diversity
    • Look everywhere for the best talent, it is evenly distributed
    • Diversity brings diversity of thought, avoids groupthink, and avoids bias
    • Diversity is just a nicer work environment

Workflow

  • Coding starts and ends with the business. Sit with the business. Understand the business problem. Eventually help implement the business change. Challenge everything, you dont have to be a business expert
  • Squads have delegated responsibility, reject command and control. Reject layers of admin and management
  • Squads are trusted to solve the business problem. No need to checkup on people. They will ask for help if they need it
  • Focus on meaninful and deep work, avoid repetitive low value work like powerpoint and xls
  • Use the best tools, build them if they dont exist

Academy

  • Capture and present thought leadership. Maybe even publish them in journals. Its your job to educate others
  • Invest in writing knowledge articles. Its better to write once read many, than waste developer time to explain things again and again
  • Link to good resources, don't reinvent the wheel
  • Do this before scaling

NHS Longivety

Twenty five more years of free at the point of use

IT has brought significant efficiencies to the industries that have embraced it. If applied correctly to the NHS, we could save billions of pounds that could be spent on patient care. The NHS was founded on the principles of universal, equitable, comprehensive, high quality, free at the point of delivery healthcare. It recently celebrated its 75th birthday and we think modern IT is at the heart of helping it get to 100 years with these principles in tact.

Productivity vs aging population

Tackling the demographic crisis

Population in the UK will decline, as it will in many western nations and even in places like China. If per capita GDP increase is less than the decrease in total population it follows that a country would experience a decline in GDP, known as an economic recession. If these conditions become permanent, the country could find itself in a permanent recession*. We can use a system of management that cuts out administration, together with AI tools, to increate the economic produtivity of individuals to compensate for population decline. Teams everywhere need to become 10% more economically productive. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_consequences_of_population_decline

Unblocking digital transformation

Quickily and cheaply replacing legacy systems

Digital Transformation is a business transformation in which we comprehensively integrate digital technologies into front-office, production, and back-office of business. Traditional enterprises are being held back from true digital transforamtion by legacy systems. We can quickily and cheaply rewrite legacy systems, brining cloud native, evergreen, zero ops applications to the enterprise.
